Cabins Structural Maintenance

Foundation, framing, weatherproofing, and interior finishes. Diagnosis complete; one disassembly will confirm full scope.

The Problem

  • Interior: water ingress has compromised walls and ceilings
  • Foundation: settling and cracks, requiring assessment once structure is exposed

Diagnosis & Timeline

  • Visual inspection: Completed March 2026
  • Disassembly: One cabin to begin immediately
  • Scope confirmation: After disassembly & measurement

Hotel Bathrooms Renovation

8 guest bathrooms: removal of outdated bathtubs, installation of modern shower systems, new flooring, fixtures, and finishes. Partially underway.

Why Now

  • Safety: Old bathtubs pose fall hazard for older guests
  • Compliance: Code now requires slip-resistant surfaces
  • Moisture: Water ingress has damaged surrounding walls
  • Market: Modern showers are guest expectation in premium lodging
Pipes — already addressed. When we tore out the bathrooms, we noticed some of the pipes were in really bad shape, clogged with sediment from the local water intake. We cleared what we could and replaced the most damaged sections. Spent so far: $2,500 (not included in the figures below).

Scale & Schedule

  • 8 hotel bathrooms in scope
  • 30 working days
  • 2 workers on site

What ties these projects together

All three address infrastructure that has already deteriorated past the point of cosmetic repair. They are not optional upgrades — each carries a concrete risk if deferred:

  • Cabins: structural — potential partial roof collapse
  • Workshop: compliance — TE1 electrical certification
  • Bathrooms: guest safety — outdated bathtubs, hazard for older guests

Two of the three are already underway. The workshop is the only one fully gated on a payment decision.
